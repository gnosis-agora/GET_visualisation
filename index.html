Rationale

The world we live in today is becoming increasingly divided in the political arena. Through research on political polarization in their book ‘Democracies Divided’, Carothers and O’Donohue (2019) expounds on how political polarization is tearing at the seams of democracies around the world, importantly highlighting the destabilizing impacts that this polarization can have on local societies and economies: legislatures roll into a gridlock, everyday societal interactions and relationships become poisoned, and hate crimes and political violence continue spiking. It is evident that in order to promote a safer, happier and more inclusive society around us, we must understand how polarization works, and how media and political discourse around us work as integral forces in shaping and reinforcing the dynamics of polarization in societies today.

While countries globally, from Brazil to India to Poland and Turkey, are fast facing severe political polarization, we have decided to narrow the focus of our analysis on the United States. Recent studies have shown that America is one of, if not, the most severely politically polarized nation in the world (Boxell et. al 2020, Carothers & O’Donohue 2019). What makes the case of the US unique is how its intense partisanship has deep societal roots and is the outcome of a profound socio-cultural struggle between conservatives and progressive visions of the country -- represented by two dominant political parties, the Democrats and the Republicans. More interestingly, America’s political divisions today date back to at least the 1950s and have been steadily intensifying for over fifty years, unlike other countries whose cases of polarization are more recent in origin. This is another important reason why we picked America, given its longer history in polarization and thus greater amount of data we could gather to work with.
